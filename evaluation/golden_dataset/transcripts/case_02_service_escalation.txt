Quantum Technologies — NCR Notification & Quality Case Experience Escalation
Attendees: Nalini (Quality PM), Yusuf (Compliance), Ravi (Director Platform Eng),
           Kenji (Client Portal Lead), Priya (VP Digital Ops)
Date: May 9

Nalini opened — said NCR and quality case notification complaints are spiking
in the PartnerPortal feedback. OEM clients tell us they didn't know an NCR
was open on their order, or worse, they got a notification and then opened
the portal only to find out the alert had been pushed to the wrong contact
on a shared enterprise account. Two client account managers in the Phoenix
region filed formal complaints last week.

------

Problem 1: NCR-ready notifications are unreliable

Today the in-portal notification fires only when the client opens the
PartnerPortal and the home screen polls the QualityHub service. If they
never open the portal, they never know the NCR or corrective action is
ready. We are leaving quality actions unactioned — and worse, clients are
finding out about non-conformances through their own receiving inspection
instead of from us.

Nalini's ask: a proactive push notification when an NCR or CAPA action is
open for the account. Kenji said the push infrastructure is in place —
this is a backend trigger problem off the QualityHub event stream, not a
portal problem.

Nalini: "And we want the notification text to include the specific NCR
number and article so the client recognises it. Like 'NCR 24Q-318:
non-conformance on Assembly P/N 7701-C is open for your review.'"

Yusuf cut in immediately — that is not OK under GDPR/CCPA and ITAR.
Surfacing a specific NCR number and a controlled article part number in
a push notification requires explicit client consent stored on the account
record, AND it can only go to the client's verified technical contact,
never the shared enterprise account default. We've been bitten by this
before.

Nalini: "Right, sorry. We can do a generic 'You have an open quality
action — open the portal for details' and let them tap through.
Detail-on-tap, not detail-in-banner."

Yusuf: "That's fine. Anything that does show the NCR number and controlled
article ID needs opt-in plus verified delivery plus a 10-year audit log
entry."

Ravi: "Let's design it both ways and flag the opt-in version as a
follow-up. Phase 1 ships generic, phase 2 adds opt-in for the
NCR-detail surface."

------

Problem 2: The shared enterprise-account problem

Today multiple technical contacts and an enterprise account admin can be
on a single client account through IdentityVault (IV). QualityHub knows
which NCR belongs to which account-ID and order, but the notification
system fires to the account, not the verified technical contact. That is
why the Phoenix client saw an NCR alert go to a procurement admin about
a quality issue assigned to the engineering reviewer.

Kenji: "We need client-contact-scoped notification delivery. The push goes
only to the device that's logged in as that specific contact identity
through IdentityVault (IV)."

Yusuf: "Yes, this is the verified contact method requirement applied to
push. It's not optional."

Ravi assigned this to Kenji and Nalini together with Yusuf as the
compliance reviewer.

------

Problem 3: SMS fallback

Nalini brought up SMS — some client contacts in field operations don't
have regular access to the portal. Could we send the quality-action
notice by SMS too?

Kenji paused. "Isn't QT-176 already for that?" Ravi checked the
backlog — yes, QT-176 is the NCR push-notification-with-SMS-fallback
ticket. It's in backlog status. Nalini: "Let's just push QT-176 up the
priority list then. No new story needed."

------

Things we explicitly said NO to this round:
- Automated voice-call reminders for NCR actions (Nalini asked, Ravi said
  no — too expensive and not requested by clients).
- Showing NCR alerts on the MES shop-floor terminal overlay while
  operators are working — out of scope for this round.

------

Next steps:
Nalini owns the proactive notification spec. Kenji owns the contact-scoped
delivery work. Yusuf will draft the GDPR/ITAR consent and audit review
checklist. QT-176 stays as the SMS workstream.
