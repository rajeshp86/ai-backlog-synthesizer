Quantum Technologies — NCR Notification & Quality Case Experience Escalation
Attendees: Nalini (Quality PM), Yusuf (Compliance), Ravi (Director Platform Eng),
           Kenji (Client Portal Lead), Priya (VP Digital Ops)
Date: May 9

Nalini opened — said NCR and quality action notification complaints are spiking
in the PartnerPortal feedback. Clients tell us they didn't know there was an open
non-conformance record on a shipment in their queue, or worse, they got a
notification and then opened the portal only to find out the alert had been sent
to the wrong person on a shared enterprise account. Two client account managers
in the Phoenix region filed formal complaints last week.

------

Problem 1: NCR-ready notifications are unreliable

Today the in-portal notification fires only when the client opens the
PartnerPortal and the home screen polls QualityHub. If they never open
the portal, they never know the NCR or corrective action is ready. We are
leaving quality actions unactioned — and worse, clients are hearing about
non-conformances from their own downstream audits instead of from us.

Nalini's ask: a proactive push notification when an NCR or corrective
action is open for the client's account. Kenji said the portal push
infrastructure is in place — this is a backend trigger problem off the
QualityHub event stream, not a client-portal problem.

Nalini: "And we want the notification text to include the specific
NCR number and article ID so the client recognizes it. Like 'NCR
QT-NCR-2024-0318 on Assembly A7-900 is open for your review.'"

Yusuf cut in immediately — that is not OK. Under GDPR/CCPA and our
Section 3 rules, surfacing a specific NCR number and controlled article
ID in a push notification requires explicit client consent stored on
the account record, AND it can only go to the client's verified
technical contact, never the shared enterprise account default. We've
been bitten by this before with ITAR-controlled assembly data.

Nalini: "Right, sorry. We can do a generic 'You have an open quality
action — open the portal for details' and let them tap through.
Detail-on-tap, not detail-in-banner."

Yusuf: "That's fine. Anything that does show the NCR number or article
ID needs opt-in plus verified delivery plus a 10-year audit log entry."

Ravi: "Let's design it both ways and flag the consent version as a
follow-up. Phase 1 ships generic, phase 2 adds opt-in for the
NCR-detail surface."

------

Problem 2: The shared enterprise-account problem

Today multiple contacts can be on a single client account through
IdentityVault (IV). QualityHub knows which NCR belongs to which
account ID and verified technical contact, but the notification
system fires to the account, not the verified contact. That is why
the Phoenix client saw an NCR alert go to a procurement contact
about an assembly assigned to a different quality engineer.

Kenji: "We need client-scoped notification delivery. The push goes
only to the contact that is logged in as that specific identity
through IdentityVault, bound to the account ID."

Yusuf: "Yes — this is the verified contact method requirement applied
to push. It's not optional."

Ravi assigned this to Kenji and Nalini together with Yusuf as the
compliance reviewer.

------

Problem 3: SMS fallback

Nalini brought up SMS — some quality engineers at client sites are
not regularly in the portal, especially on the plant floor. Could we
send the NCR-ready notice by SMS too?

Kenji paused. "Isn't QT-176 already for that?" Ravi checked the
backlog — yes, QT-176 is the NCR push notification ticket and already
carries the SMS fallback scope. It's in backlog status. Nalini:
"Let's just push QT-176 up the priority list then. No new story needed."

------

Things we explicitly said NO to this round:
- Automated voice-call reminders for NCR actions (Nalini asked,
  Ravi said no — too expensive and not requested by clients).
- Surfacing NCR alerts on the MES shop-floor terminal display —
  out of scope for this round and a distraction concern on the
  production floor.

------

Next steps:
Nalini owns the proactive notification spec. Kenji owns the
client-scoped delivery work. Yusuf will draft the GDPR/ITAR
consent and audit review checklist. QT-176 stays as the SMS workstream.
