Yusuf: Three findings from the audit.

1. IEC 62443 firmware signing attestation — our annual evidence collection for the firmware deployment pipeline lapsed. 2. ITAR controlled technical data residency — the analytics team has been replicating ITAR-controlled article technical data (part drawings + test specifications) into an EU-West data lake for a partner reporting project. Per ITAR/EAR, controlled technical data must reside in US-East and US-West regions. Cross-region replication to EU/APAC without an export licence is forbidden. We need to stop the replication and re-region the affected data immediately.

The older NCR records need to be migrated to long-term storage and the retention policy updated in QualityHub before the next AS9100 surveillance audit.
