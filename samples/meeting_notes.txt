Digital Operations — Q3 Planning Meeting
Attendees: Priya (VP Digital Operations), Ravi (Director Platform Engineering),
           Kenji (Client Portal Lead), Petra (Supply Chain & Operations Tech),

Priya kicked off — said the last 90 days of feedback from enterprise
client admins, quality engineers, and the PartnerPortal survey are telling

synthesized list of client-facing problems we should tackle in Q3.

------

Theme 1: Firmware deployment failures during maintenance windows

Petra led on this. Last month was the worst — three regional batch firmware
deployments to embedded control modules at client sites failed during the
nightly maintenance window. Modules sat in "Update Pending" for over 45
minutes, blocking the next scheduled deployment wave. Clients reported that
embedded controllers were unresponsive during the stuck state. Petra's data:
when the FirmwareVault CDN throughput cap is hit, the deployment orchestrator
keeps retrying without backing off — it can't even fall back gracefully
because it can't validate the partial download locally.

She wants us to build a proper staged-rollout with offline resilience for
the firmware deployment system. At minimum: modules should resume normal
operation immediately if an update stalls, retries should use exponential
back-off, and the rollout throttle should be configurable per deployment
ring. She thinks auto-rollback would be ideal but compliance is going to
push back. (Yusuf confirmed — IEC 62443 and our firmware signing policy
require a validated rollback path, not an automatic one for security-critical
firmware updates.)

Ravi asked: "Can we checkpoint the download progress on the module so
when connectivity resumes it picks up where it left off instead of
restarting the full package?" Petra: "Yes, that's the design. Local
staging partition on the controller, resume from byte offset when online."

Priya: "This is high priority. We're seeing client satisfaction drop
after failed deployment nights and account managers are escalating."

------

Theme 2: PartnerPortal order status showing stale data

Kenji has been deep in the feedback. OEM clients search for their order
status in the PartnerPortal and the top results show orders marked "On Track"
that are actually behind in production. They click through and can't get
real status. NPS for clients who hit this is -28. Clients who don't hit
it are +18.

Three things tangled here:
- Order status data in the PartnerPortal is refreshed only every 4 hours
- The "On Track" badge pulls from a third-party ERP feed that disagrees
  with real-time MES data about 18% of the time
- Alternative delivery window options don't appear until after the client
  has already escalated to their account manager, way too late

Kenji: "I think we want to push live production telemetry from the MES into
the order status service as a signal. And surface alternative delivery
windows inline when an order shows high latency risk."

Yusuf raised: "We need to make sure we're not showing differential status
to different users for ITAR-controlled article orders — we can't expose
controlled production data without access control gating."

Priya: "Note that for ITAR review. Don't let it block us — gate by
article classification."

------

Theme 3: NCR / CAPA case intake channel split

Nalini brought receipts. The single biggest complaint to the client support
line is "I submitted an NCR via the PartnerPortal but the quality engineer
says they have no record and nothing has been actioned." It's because we
have TWO case intake channels: the PartnerPortal submission, and direct
QualityHub entry by account managers. They write to different systems and
the systems don't reconcile in real time.

Nalini wants:
- One unified case record per client-order/incident, regardless of intake channel
- A clear status the client sees ("submitted / in review / resolved")
- A push notification when the corrective action is actually confirmed

Yusuf: "GDPR and ITAR. Any notification that references a specific NCR
number and controlled article description has to be consent-based and we
can only send to the client's verified technical contact — not a shared
enterprise account."

------

Theme 4: Supplier delivery confirmation confusion

Priya mentioned this almost as an aside but it kept coming up. Internal
production planners and clients don't understand how Quantum Technologies
handles supplier delivery confirmations against production schedules.
They get a "Materials Confirmed" alert at PO placement and then days later
"Material Delayed — production rescheduled" and they're upset because they
didn't realise the initial confirmation was provisional. The confirmed
delivery date isn't visible from any client-facing surface.

Kenji thinks the fix is mostly informational — show a "material status"
card in the PartnerPortal that makes the confirmed delivery date, outstanding
PO status, and production impact visible. Ravi: "Should be a small addition.
Not a platform rewrite. We can fit it in."

------

Theme 5: Legacy Gen1 embedded controller hardware in 2019–2021 vintage

Petra: side-bar but worth flagging. Gen1 embedded controllers in the
2019–2021 vintage are running with 256 MB flash. They can't receive
differential firmware packages — only full firmware images — and they don't
support TLS 1.3. Hardware refresh is approved for the next device campaign
but won't reach full coverage until FY26 Q2.

Ravi: "Good call-out. Flag this on every new firmware-dependent story —
the Gen1 controller has a different capability floor than Gen2 devices."

------

Theme 6: Remote diagnostics unreliable on industrial plant Wi-Fi

Kenji again. The PartnerPortal's remote diagnostic commands for field-deployed
modules succeed only about 88% of the time on industrial plant Wi-Fi, and
the worst offenders are congested plant networks and high-interference
factory floors. The portal shows "Command Sent" the moment it queues, so
clients think it worked and come back to a module that never received the
command. Two things: (1) the portal must reflect the *actual* acknowledgement
from the diagnostic control plane, not just that the command was queued;
(2) the actuation path should go through the diagnostic control plane with a
validated message authentication code rather than the legacy path.

Petra: "Clients are calling support for what is really a failed remote
command. It's expensive and embarrassing."

Priya: "High priority. This is a trust issue."

------

Theme 7: PartnerPortal sign-in friction

Petra raised this for the client side. Client admin accounts at our larger
client groups juggle separate logins for the PartnerPortal, and sessions
expire mid-order-write so they lose a partially entered order record. The
top-20 client groups want single sign-on, and security wants two-factor
on the portal because it touches controlled-article data.

Ravi: "Both of these are about PartnerPortal identity. Make sure any new
auth flow federates through IdentityVault (IV) — we are not standing up a
second credential store."
